In the process I attended some talks on REST which I found interesting and challenging.

The first was "Web Development: You're Doing it Wrong" by Stefan Tilkov. He was taking aim at two trends in web development. Fairly uncontroversially he was saying that server-side component based web frameworks like old school JSF which are heavily stateful are a bad idea. More controversially he was arguing that client side component based web frameworks, or Single Page Applications, such as those one might make with Ember, AngularJS or Knockout are essentially making the same mistake just shifting it to the client. He was arguing that we should be embracing the things that HTTP and the browser gives us for free, like the forward & back buttons, URIs that identify exactly where we are and are able to be bookmarked / passable around, and the possibility for people on less capable clients (right down to Lynx or Curl) to still be able to interact fully with the application.

These principles are expressed in the ROCA (Resource Oriented Client Architecture) style recommendations that he's heavily involved with.
