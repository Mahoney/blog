--- layout: post title: Ordnance Survey iOS App - Poor Distribution Decisions date: '2013-05-16T00:59:00.000+01:00' author: Robert Elliot tags: modified_time: '2013-05-16T00:59:37.705+01:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-8805447266344101474.post-1160874615929668301 blogger_orig_url: http://blog.lidalia.org.uk/2013/05/ordnance-survey-ios-app-poor.html --- This is borderline off-topic, but the app is software and design decisions around the route to market for software is close enough for me to put it here.

I've downloaded the Ordnance Survey app for the iPad. For non-UK readers, the Ordnance Survey produce some of the world's best mapping examples, covering the whole of mainland Britain at 1:25,000 scale which basically shows every building. For anyone who does any walking in the countryside these are brilliant. We've got tons of the paper ones.

The app's pretty nice - performs well, lets you do routes. However, it suffers from two fatal business decisions:

Content is over-priced


Content is non-transferable
