--- layout: post title: Accessing Jetty on Ubuntu from another machine date: '2010-04-21T23:13:00.003+01:00' author: Robert Elliot tags: modified_time: '2010-04-21T23:22:37.654+01:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-8805447266344101474.post-142373186234879456 blogger_orig_url: http://blog.lidalia.org.uk/2010/04/ok-so-following-on-from-my-previous.html --- OK, so following on from my previou. blog post I thought I'd do some performance testing to see ho. Jetty compares with Apache. Installed VirtualBox on my Mac, downloade. and installed ubuntu 9.04 server edition with bridge networking so . could access it from the host - all painless. Installed apache2 . painless. Installed jmeter on the mac - painless. Ran some performanc. tests. Looked to repeat the experiment against Jetty - aargh!. Just wasted 3 hours trying to work out why I couldn't connect to Jett. from the host. Turns out that the documentation, in Debian/Ubuntu a. least, is wrong - when /etc/init.d/jetty claims that leaving JETTY_HOS. blank will result in accepting connections from all hosts it's lying. You need to set it to 0.0.0.0 or it will only be accessible fro. localhost (127.0.0.1). You can tell this because if you do a netstat -a. you will see this: 
Prot. . . . .  Local Addres. . . . . . . . . State
tcp. .   . .  0 127.0.0.1:8080 :::. . . . . . . LISTEN
 that means the bind-address is localhost so the process is inaccessible outside the server. It should look like this: 
Prot. . . . .  Loca. Addres. Foreign Addres. . .  State
tcp. .   . .  0 :::808. . .  :::. . . . . . . LISTEN
 Hope this helps some other poor confused person out there, took me a long time to Google this... there's a Debian issue here: http://bugs.debian.org/cgi-bin/bugreport.cgi?bug=554874
